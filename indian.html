Indian Mathematicians

Indian mathematicians have contributed profoundly to number theory, algebra, trigonometry, and astronomical calculations. From the discovery of zero and the decimal system to the creation of infinite series, their work has shaped both ancient and modern mathematics.

1. Pingala (c. 3rd–2nd century BCE)
2. Aryabhata I (476–550)
3. Varāhamihira (505–587)
4. Brahmagupta (598–c. 668)
5. Bhāskara I (c. 600–c. 680)
6. Mahāvīra (fl. c. 850)
7. Śrīpati (c. 1019–c. 1066)
8. Jayadeva (fl. 11th–12th century)
9. Bhāskara II (1114–c. 1185)
10. Mādhava of Sangamagrama (c. 1340–c. 1425)
11. Nīlakaṇṭha Somayāji (1444–1544)
12. Jyeṣṭhadeva (c. 1500–c. 1575)
13. Achyuta Pisharati (1550–1621)
14. Melpathur Nārāyaṇa Bhattathiri (1560–1646)
15. Radhanath Sikdar (1813–1870)
16. Ashutosh Mukherjee (1864–1924)
17. Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887–1920)
18. P. C. Mahalanobis (1893–1972)
19. Raj Chandra Bose (1901–1987)
20. T. Vijayaraghavan (1902–1955)
21. C. R. Rao (1920–2023)
22. Harish-Chandra (1923–1983)
23. Ataullah Rashidi (20th century)
24. Mahadevan (dates not provided)
